# Contributor Rewards & Support

When you contribute to **nexu**, we want it to be more than just "PR merged" — it also means:

- Every sincere effort you make is seen
- Your contribution doesn't just live in the merge history
- Your growth is continuously recorded and accumulated
- You feel welcomed even on your first contribution

## What Support We Provide

For `good-first-issue` and first-time contributors, we try to provide:

- **Clearer task scope**: We break tasks into small, clear, verifiable pieces
- **More specific review feedback**: If changes are needed, we explain why and suggest direction
- **First-time contribution guidance**: We provide necessary context and advice for newcomers
- **Public appreciation & points**: After merge, you enter the contributor appreciation and points flow

If you haven't started yet, check out:

- [Good First Issue Contributor Guide](/guide/first-pr)
- [Contributing](/guide/contributing)
- [Good First Issue List](https://github.com/nexu-io/nexu/labels/good-first-issue)

## Where Your Contributions Are Displayed

When your contribution is merged, it will be shown in these places:

1. **GitHub README**: Contributor display with **daily, monthly, and all-time leaderboards**
2. **Discord [Start your first Nexu PR](https://discord.gg/vMrySTJW8u)**: Bot auto-publishes leaderboard updates

## Current Points System

Points are based on the Issue label, keeping it simple and clear:

| Issue Label | Points | Equivalent Value |
| --- | --- | --- |
| `help wanted` | 2,000 | ≈ $20 USD nexu usage credits |
| `good first issue` | 500 | ≈ $5 USD nexu usage credits |
| Other (no label above) | 500 | ≈ $5 USD nexu usage credits |

### How Points Are Awarded

1. **Contributor submits a PR** linked to the corresponding Issue
2. **The dev team reviews** the PR for code quality and completeness
3. **After the PR is merged**, the team confirms and awards points to your account

> Points are awarded only after the dev team has reviewed and merged your PR. The team reserves the final right of interpretation on point awards.

## What Can You Do with Points

Points can be directly redeemed for **nexu product usage credits**, and also serve as proof of your community contributions:

- Points = real product usage credits — contribute and get rewarded
- First-time contributors receive clear positive feedback
- Long-term contributors build visible identity in the community
- Point records become part of your open-source contribution resume
